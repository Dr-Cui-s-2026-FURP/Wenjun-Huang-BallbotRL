## April 1, 2026

### Paper 1: PPO (Schulman et al. 2017)

**Key Findings:**
- PPO's clipped surrogate objective prevents destructively large policy updates
- $L^{CLIP}(\theta) = \hat{\mathbb{E}}_t[\min(r_t(\theta)\hat{A}_t, \text{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1-\epsilon, 1+\epsilon)\hat{A}_t)]$ with $\epsilon=0.2$ works best
- Enables multiple epochs of updates on same data → better sample efficiency
- Simpler to implement than TRPO while achieving similar stability

**Relevance to Ballbot:** Safe policy updates are critical for hardware deployment

---

### Paper 2: Hwangbo et al. - ANYmal Locomotion (Science Robotics 2019)

**Key Findings:**
- Trained policies in simulation → deployed directly to real quadruped with no fine-tuning
- **Actuator Net**: Neural network (3 hidden layers, 32 units) learns command-to-torque mapping
    - Validation error: 0.74 Nm vs ideal model's 3.55 Nm
- **Domain Randomization**: mass ±15%, friction ±30%, COM ±2cm
- **Reward design**: logistic kernel for velocity tracking, penalties for torque, joint speed, foot slip
- **Key insight**: Joint state history (t, t-0.01, t-0.02s) essential for contact detection

**Relevance to Ballbot:** Actuator Net approach applicable to omniwheel-motor system; history-based observations useful for ball contact detection

## April 2, 2026

### Paper 3: Tobin et al. - Domain Randomization (2017)

**Key Findings:**
- Core hypothesis validated: sufficient simulation variability → generalization to real world with no additional training
- Non-realistic textures (random RGB, gradients, checkerboard) are sufficient
- Performance degrades significantly with <1,000 unique textures (Figure 5)
- Camera position randomization (±10cm, ±0.1 rad) improves robustness
- Distractors in training critical for clutter resilience (Table II: 7.2cm error without distractors vs 1.8cm with)

**Relevance to Ballbot:** Friction, mass, and sensor noise randomization will be essential for Sim2Real transfer

---

### Paper 4: OpenAI - Dexterous Manipulation (2018)

**Key Findings:**
- LSTM policies implicitly identify environment parameters (80% accuracy predicting object size)
- Memory crucial for handling partial observability (Table 5: LSTM median 13 successes vs FF policy 3.5)
- Distributed training (384 workers, 8 GPUs) generates 2 years of experience per hour
- Randomizations critical: without physics randomization → median 0 successes (Table 4)

**Relevance to Ballbot:** LSTM policies recommended for Ballbot's partially observable dynamics; large-scale parallel training accelerates development
